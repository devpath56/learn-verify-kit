# Certification Gate

A concept, answer, or explanation is "ready" only when it scores **>= 4.0 / 5.0 weighted** across the four dimensions below. Each dimension comes from one of the kit's four verbs, so certification is just the four skills reporting in.

| Dimension | Source verb | Scored 1–5 by |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Recall** | `learn` | Can the learner reproduce it cold, in their own words, on a NEW case? 5 = fluent on a novel case; 1 = can't recall unaided. |
| **Grill Depth** | `understand` | Panel-minimum L0–L5, mapped 1–5. 5 = survives the domain-expert edge-case attack; 1 = breaks on the first clarifying question. |
| **Insight** | `clarify` | Highest grounded+actionable insight level reached, L0–L5 mapped 1–5. 5 = a generative reframe; 1 = restatement only. |
| **Clarity** | `revise` | Recall Rubric pass rate. 5 = all 10 checks pass; drop a point per failed check, floor at 1. |

## Weighting

Default equal weight (each 25%). Adjust for the use case:
- **Learning a concept to retain it:** Recall 40 / Clarity 30 / Grill 20 / Insight 10.
- **Preparing to defend a position:** Grill 40 / Insight 30 / Recall 20 / Clarity 10.
- **Sharpening a brainstorm:** Insight 50 / Clarity 25 / Grill 15 / Recall 10.

## The rule

- **>= 4.0 weighted -> certified.** Ready to use, teach, or ship.
- **< 4.0 -> not ready.** Name the lowest dimension and route to its verb (low Recall -> `learn` again; low Grill -> `understand`; low Insight -> `clarify`; low Clarity -> `revise`).

Do not certify on a strong average that hides a 1. If any single dimension is at 1, it is not ready regardless of the weighted total — a concept you can't recall, or that collapses on the first hard question, fails no matter how clear the prose is.
